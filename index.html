- Grocery Retail Data Analysis (using R)

Since September 2015, I have been working as a research assistant focusing on retail data analytics at UC Berkeley. This repository demonstrates parts of analysis we conducted on the Dominick's scanner data. The database covers store-level scanner data collected at Dominick's Finer Foods over a period of more than seven years (1989-1997). Our goal is to build a model that can best describe the relationship between price, promotion, and sales of different products

- Used Car Price Prediction (using Python)

This is the final project for Data Analytics course at UC Berkeley. Our team focuses on the used car market in Germany. We act as a used car dealer in Germany and analyze the most recent data on ebay to do a basic analysis on used car price and factors that have significant influence on it. My mian controbution is python programming, which includes cleaning data, building model, and visualzations. Please refer to the repository for more details.

- Optimization Case Study (using Python)

This repository illustrates a capaciated lot sizing problem of a door sub-assembly lines in a worldwide home appliance maker's refrigerator factory. The main goal is to determine lot sizes and production sequence for the factory's mixed-models assembly line. A mathematical formulation is discussed in the paper and a python script for Gurobi solver is uploaded.

- Time Series Analysis (using R)

This is an individual project for the course Introduction to Time Series Analysis taught at Statistics Department, UC Berkeley. You can find the paper and codes at this repository.

Summary of the paper:

  • Applied intervention analysis and fitted SARIMA model to monthly Taiwanese visitors travelling to the USA over a period of nineteen years.
  • Conducted Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) with R: plotted periodogram to examine frequency in the series, decomposed the series through loess method, and inspected the cross-correlation function of two correlated series.
